ABOUT

A Centralized, Noise Free Dashboard for Monitoring Defense and Geopolitical Situations.


MOTIVATION

I loved monitoring military and geopolitical situations as they unfold. However, browsing on Twitter (X), Media Outlets, and YouTube channels became increasingly "Distracting" for me. Despite my best efforts, the "NOISE" on these platforms made it difficult to stay focused.

I wanted a centralized, organized place where I could monitor developments without the distractions of algorithms or social media bloat. That is why I built this platform. It give you snapshot information at your fingertips so you can quickly monitor the situation and deep-dive only into what truly matters.

USAGE

The platform gets updated every 60 minutes. This ensures the data remains current without hitting the limitations of the free hosting tiers that keep this project sustainable.

To keep the experience fast and relevant, the platform only displays information from the past 48 hours. Older data is not stored in any form; it is discarded to ensure the dashboard remains a "pulse" of the current situation.

CONTENT

This is an aggregator website; I do not own any of the content presented here. All copyrights belong to their respective owners.

Everyone has specific preferences for media outlets, Twitter handles, or YouTube channels. The built-in Source Filter allows you to curate the feed to your specific needs, making the tool useful for a wider audience interested in defense and geopolitics.

SUPPORT

This is purely volunteer-based software. While it will always remain free to use, the quality of the feed depends on the data it consumes. I aim to make the list of outlets as exhaustive as possible, but I cannot do it without your support.

How you can help:

  • Suggestions: If I’ve missed a major news outlet or a specific analyst, youtube channel, please suggest them. You can reach out via Contact.
  • Sponsorship: If you wish to sponsor the project, your contributions could help secure a custom domain so users don't have to remember a long GitHub link.
